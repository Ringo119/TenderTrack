A Feature I'd Add That Excel Can't Easily Do
When you create a new job, the app could estimate your workload based on the fee and type of project. For example:

Small domestic extension (£300–£500): 2–3 days

Loft conversion (£600–£900): 4–5 days

New build (£1,500+): 10–15 days

It would automatically place the work into your planner, and you could simply drag it if needed. This would make scheduling far quicker than colouring cells manually.

Another Valuable Addition
A dedicated Documents tab on each job would let you keep everything together:

Tender documents

Drawings

Bills of Quantities

Quotes

Emails (or exported PDFs)

Completed invoice

With that, every project becomes a single digital folder rather than a collection of files and spreadsheets.

I think this approach would give you an app that feels familiar because it follows the way you already work, while eliminating repetitive data entry and keeping your planning, invoicing, and records all in one place.
